Economic VCG Auction Approach For NOMA Wireless Relay Networks üì±

  • Context: In NOMA networks, stronger-signal users can decode and forward packets to weaker users as relay devices.
  • Problem: Traditional NOMA doesn't effectively utilize decoded and cached content for network optimization.
  • Solution: Economic auction-based NOMA relay approach using Vickrey-Clarke-Groves (VCG) auction theory.
  • Mechanism: Optimal relay-user matching based on utility models for base station and relay devices.
  • Results: Maximized utility for relay devices and improved service quality for user equipment.
Keywords: QoE, QoS, Wireless Networks, Multimedia Streaming, Network Optimization

Uberization of NOMA Wireless Network Resource Sharing: A Driver-Passenger Game-Theoretic Approachüì±

  • Concept: A ride-sharing inspired pricing scheme for NOMA wireless networks with high spectral efficiency.
  • Model: "Driver" and "passenger" users share power, time frame and frequency spectrum resources.
  • Mechanism: Passengers pay virtual payment; drivers receive rewards after paying commission to base station.
  • Analysis: Utility functions based on wireless multimedia quality with Nash Equilibrium optimization.
  • Results: Improved system utility performance compared to traditional orthogonal multiple access pricing.
Keywords: QoE, QoS, Wireless Networks, Multimedia Streaming, Network Optimization
